# Gaia BH1: joint RV + Gaia epoch astrometry

Gaia BH1 (El-Badry et al. 2023, MNRAS 518, 1057) is a 0.93 Msun G star in
a 185.6-day, e = 0.45 orbit around a dark 9.62 Msun black hole at 480 pc
-- discovered from its Gaia astrometric orbit and confirmed with RVs.

```
poetry run exozippy gaiabh1.yaml
```

## Data

- `GaiaBH1.RV.rv`: the 41 REAL published radial velocities (LAMOST, MagE,
  HIRES, GMOS, X-shooter, FEROS, ESI; El-Badry et al. 2023, Table 4).
  All instruments were calibrated to a common zeropoint to ~0.1 km/s, so
  they are fit as a single instrument with one gamma.
- `GaiaBH1.GaiaDR4sim.astrom`: SIMULATED Gaia along-scan epoch astrometry
  (the real epoch data are not public until DR4).  Generated by
  `simulate_gaia_astrometry.py`:
  - scan times, scan position angles, and along-scan parallax factors from
    the REAL Gaia scanning law (GOST forecast for BH1's position, cached
    in `gost_bh1_transits.csv`), restricted to the nominal DR4 window;
  - photocenter orbit from the published joint solution;
  - 0.2 mas Gaussian noise per FoV transit (approximately the DR3-era
    per-transit AL precision at G = 13.8).

  The script validates the along-scan parallax factors computed from the
  Gaia spacecraft ephemeris (`src/exozippy/ephemerides/gaia.eph`, JPL
  Horizons) against GOST's own values (they agree to < 1e-4).

## Model

The `astrometryinstrument` component (`mode: gaia`) models each abscissa

    w = dE(t) sin(psi) + dN(t) cos(psi)

where (dE, dN) contain position offsets from the catalog reference,
proper motion, parallax (with the Gaia spacecraft ephemeris), and the
photocenter orbit of every companion (EXOFASTv2 conventions: omega_*;
bigomega = PA of the ascending node, East of North, where the star
recedes; consistent with the RV sign convention, so the joint fit breaks
the (Omega, omega) -> (Omega+180, omega+180) degeneracy that astrometry
alone leaves).

Expected recovery: the joint fit lands close to, but not exactly on, the
published joint solution, because the REAL RVs are in mild (~1 sigma)
tension with the simulated astrometry (which encodes the joint solution):
the posterior pulls omega and e to El-Badry's RV-only values (omega =
13.9 deg, e = 0.447), dragging correlated ~2 sigma shifts in M_BH,
parallax, and cosi.  A reference run gives Omega = 93.7 +/- 2.2,
omega = 14.0 +/- 1.1, e = 0.447 +/- 0.005, M_BH = 8.6 +/- 0.3 Msun with
~0.5% divergences, r_hat = 1.00, and bulk ESS > 5000 on all parameters.
